UNIT 15: TOM OCKERSE

WHAT IS GRAPHIC DESIGN?

Assignment 1

PECHA KUCHA

I created a presentation about where I am as a graphic designer and where I would like to be going.

Reflection
The creation of these pecha kuchas was not my favorite way to spend a day. However, after listening to everyone's presentations and uncovering connections between the different subject matter, most people were able to stretch the question "what is graphic design?" into something pretty interesting. For myself, I chose to approach my questions with a subtle humor, using only Google Image-sourced memes to populate my backgrounds. After having taken a step back, I think the pecha kucha presentation mode is a graphic design constraint and tool I would like to revisit.

Assignment 2a

GRAPHIC DESIGN SCRUBS

I created a pair of graphic design scrubs as a way to frame and deepen the conversation of graphic designers as service providers. These served partly as a hyperbolic and humorous approach to the subject as well as a way for people to be up front about what matters to them within the field of graphic design.

Reflection
Looking back, this part element of Unit 15 was very fun for me to complete and allowed me to work in a medium I’ve always wanted to try: clothing. That being said, it doesn’t really back the same punch as the other elements of the project and worked more as a moment of comedic releif during the final walk about.

Assignment 2b

CODE OF ETHICS FOR GRAPHIC DESIGN CAREGIVERS

I created a code of ethics for graphic designers. It is directly based off of the United States Nurses Code of Ethics and unveils some interesting parallels and questions.

Reflection
This part of the project, for me was the most interesting because it seemed to surprise people most. It was one of those moments where a quick fast move produces really lovely and compelling results. I was also really excited to try sewing a booklet on a sewing machine, a technique I've always wanted to try.

UNIT 16: DIANE LEE

WORKING TOGETHER

Assignment 1

COLLABORATION COMPENDIUM

I wrote my essay and designed an eight page booklet on Walter Keane, husband of Margaret Keane whom he plagiarized paintings from. They were an example, on an obvious level, of how collaborations could fall apart.

Reflection
Personally, I think this was an interesting project when knowing that it would appear all together. However, I found myself pretty frustrated by the timing of it. It felt like a project that was specifically for the department in general rather than an exercise that really helped us learn a lot about collaboration or even book design.

Workshop

ANTI ANTI ALIASING

I created piece of a larger map for the workshop as well as a side-by-side piece on lock picking for the workshop class site.

Reflection
Perhaps it is more of a personal problem, but I've always really struggled with day workshops. I never feel like I'm actually able to complete the work to my satisfaction. In this case, I felt at an even greater disadvantage because of the web-based aspects. It was still a fun and informative presentaiton, however, I'm not sure the workshop aspect brought me a whole lot closer to understanding.

Assignment 2

FORMING BDEHD

Julian, Jack, Arthur and I came together in a collaborative unit to create the Bureau of Design and Experimental Health Development. We chose to work together because of our different skill sets and our desire to learn from one another.

Reflection
At first, I was a little reticent to work in a group of four because of scheduling conflicts and the possibility that we wouldn't work well together. After things got started, however, it worked out very well given the scope of work we wanted to produce and the positive way we were all able to work together.
